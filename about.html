Founder's Story

Before solar, Tivi spent over a decade building some of the largest industrial power plants in the world — projects at a scale most people only see on a map. He understood infrastructure. He understood scale. What he didn't have, when he eventually walked into his first solar sales job in California, was a single person willing to show him how any of it worked.

No playbook. No mentor. No commission sheet. Just the field, and whatever he could figure out on his own. He figured out a lot. Enough to build his own solar company and hire the kind of people he'd wished he had access to early on.

That's when the pattern became impossible to ignore. Even experienced reps — closers with real mileage behind them — couldn't calculate their own commission. When a deal included an adder, a battery system, or a roofwork package, they had to ask. And Tivi started wondering why that was the case for almost everyone who came through his doors.

“

Most owners keep the math murky on purpose. If your rep has to ask what they make on a deal, you hold the leverage — and you can quietly shape that number in your favor.

The ambiguity wasn't a training gap. It was a management strategy. Commission structures around adders, batteries, and roofwork were deliberately complex. Reps who had to ask what they made were reps who could be redirected — and quietly underpaid. The confusion wasn't accidental. For many owners and managers, it was the business model.

The Decision That Built This

Tivi decided that was wrong — not just ethically, but strategically. You cannot build a team of high performers by keeping them in the dark. Transparency isn't generosity. It's the minimum you owe someone who is giving their career to your company. So he built TiviForge — and opened everything up.

TiviForge Academy is one place where any rep, first week or five years in, gets every answer they need without going through a manager with a conflict of interest. Commission calculators. Deal structures. NEM 3.0. Adder breakdowns. Battery sizing. Compliance. All of it — transparent, accurate, and yours.

CSLB HIS Requirement

All 1099 independent contractors who solicit, negotiate, or sell home improvement contracts in California must obtain and maintain valid CSLB Home Improvement Salesperson (HIS) registration. TiviForge provides guidance but does not guarantee approval. CSLB.gov →

NEM 3.0 Accuracy

Net Billing Tariff (NEM 3.0) rules, export rates, and incentives are current as of March 2026. Subject to change by CPUC at any time. Always verify current rates with the applicable utility and CPUC.

Solar Disclosure

California law requires the Solar Energy System Disclosure Document be provided to homeowners in 16-pt bold before signing. The Home Solicitation Sales Act provides a 3-day right of rescission. TiviForge provides compliance training — all reps are responsible for following applicable law.

Independent Contractor Status

All TiviForge sales representatives operate as 1099 independent contractors under California law. TiviForge does not employ representatives directly. Reps are responsible for their own tax obligations, licensing, and compliance.
